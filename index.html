Generational Legacy Assessment

A structured documentation programme that produces a contemporaneous, clinician-signed record of a patient's capacity, intentions, and family context — designed to withstand retrospective scrutiny in trust, estate, and capacity proceedings.

The evidentiary gap this addresses

In trust and estate matters, capacity is assessed at the point of drafting. The evidentiary vulnerability arises later — when that assessment is tested under cross-examination or subjected to retrospective scrutiny in proceedings, sometimes years after the original instructions were taken. A standard clinical consultation, however thorough, produces a limited contemporaneous record. The Generational Legacy Assessment is designed specifically to close that gap.

"The question in contested capacity matters is rarely whether an assessment was conducted. It is whether the record produced at the time of the patient's decision is sufficiently detailed, independently witnessed, and clinically defensible to resist challenge."

What the assessment produces

The assessment comprises three structured documentation sessions, concluding in a formal clinical report. These are not clinical or psychiatric assessments in the conventional sense. They are professionally conducted documentation sessions, the outputs of which are designed to be referenced in proceedings.

Capacity baseline

Clinician-assessed record of the patient's mental capacity at a defined point in time, signed by a Senior Consultant Psychiatrist.

Contemporaneous intentions record

Structured documentation of the patient's legacy intentions — in their own voice, at the time of their decisions, witnessed and transcribed.

Family context mapping

A documented record of the relational landscape at the time of the patient's decisions — observed and recorded by the clinician, not self-reported.

Formal clinical report

Litigation-ready synthesis of all sessions. Signed by Dr Joseph Leong, Senior Consultant Psychiatrist, Promises Healthcare.

Evidentiary value for your practice

Strengthens the documentary record at the point of drafting

Capacity challenges typically succeed where the contemporaneous record is thin or relies on a single brief consultation. A three-session assessment with a formal signed report materially raises the evidentiary threshold any challenger must meet.

Addresses the retrospective scrutiny problem

When instructions are scrutinised months or years later, the clinician's ability to speak to what was observed — not merely what was reported — is significant. The assessment is structured to produce exactly this kind of independently witnessed record.

Documents the family context, not just the patient

Undue influence and testamentary capacity challenges frequently turn on the relational dynamics present at the time of the patient's decisions. The family mapping session produces a clinician-observed record of those dynamics — a category of evidence not produced by standard capacity assessments.

Suitable for referral prior to execution of key instruments

The assessment is most effective when conducted before or concurrent with the execution of wills, LPAs, trust instruments, and significant inter vivos transfers — creating a contemporaneous record aligned to the legal instructions you are taking.
